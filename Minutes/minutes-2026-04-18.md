### Navigation Styles Add-on Proposal (@chennes)

*   **Current State:** FreeCAD has many built-in navigation styles, but they are mostly boilerplate copy-paste code with little unique implementation.
*   **Proposal:** To make navigation styles implementable in Python, so they can become Addons and thus to reduce core code complexity. Gesture navigation style will remain in C++ due to its complexity.
*   **Development:** Code for a Python navigation style add-on has already been started.
*   **API:** A clear, documented API for add-ons is recommended.

### AI Policy Discussion (@chennes/@yorik)

*   **Yorik's Stance:** Yorik, a project founder, lead maintainer, and public face of FreeCAD, is proposing to resign due to strong disagreements about the project's openness to AI. He feels his concerns are not being heard by the maintainers. @chennes requested a chance to speak on his behalf to try to achieve a community consensus on a "No AI" policy, and presented the following: 
*   **AI Concerns:**
    *   **Legal Risks:** AI can unknowingly steal code, leading to license violations and breaches of community trust. AI-generated code is currently not copyrightable.
    *   **Community Trust:** Using AI-generated code violates the social contract of open source and can erode community trust.
    *   **Code Quality & Technical Debt:** AI-generated code can introduce complexity, bugs, and may lead to developers' skills atrophying. It exacerbates technical debt.
    *   **GitHub Dependency:** Reliance on GitHub's AI-enhanced environment and potential for bot spam creates a liability.
    *   **Ethical Considerations:** AI tools are often developed by companies that don't have FreeCAD's best interests at heart. AI shows no respect for existing human-written code. Significant environmental concerns exacerbate all of the other problem.
*   **Proposed Policy:**
    *   **Statement of Principles:** FreeCAD is made by and for humans, and should remain that way. Contributions should be authored and presented by humans.
    *   **Four Pillars:**
        1.  No AI-generated code.
        2.  No AI-generated commit messages.
        3.  No AI-generated PR descriptions.
        4.  No AI-generated responses to reviewer feedback.
    *   **Implementation:** Debate within the community, take the proposal to user-focused venues (like the forum), potentially add to `CONTRIBUTING.md`, modify the PR template with a checkbox, restrict PR creation to the developers group, and begin reducing CI usage to eventually migrate off GitHub.
*   **Community Feedback:**
    *   **Support for Policy:** Many developers agreed that AI is a problem and radical action is needed.
    *   **Concerns about Strictness:** Some feel the policy is too strict, especially regarding tool usage vs. contribution content, and that AI is a "gradient" not a binary issue. Auto-complete versus full agentic code generation, etc.
    *   **Defining "AI-Generated":** A need was expressed to clearly define what constitutes AI-generated code to avoid ambiguity.
    *   **Human Responsibility:** Some felt that emphasis should be placed on the contributor's responsibility for their code, regardless of AI assistance.
    *   **Survey Results:** The developer community is split on an outright ban on "AI-generated code", though a very large spike of "Neutral" survey responses could indicate a lack of clarity about what exactly that phrase meant. However, there is strong support for prohibiting AI responses to reviewer feedback, and strong (but not universal) support for banning AI-generated PR descriptions/commit messages.
*   **Next Steps:**
    *   Continue discussion to attempt to reach consensus among developers and to refine the language used in the policy. Once the language is clear, reach out to other constituents to hear their opinions.
